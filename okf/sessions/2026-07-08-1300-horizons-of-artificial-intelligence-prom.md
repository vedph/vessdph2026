# Abstract

This session explores the dynamic intersection of artificial intelligence, climate disinformation, and eco-anxiety, set against the backdrop of rapid digital innovation and shifting sociocultural landscapes. Recent academic research reveals how AI shapes the spread of climate narratives and influences emotional responses—fueling both uncertainty and creativity. Taking the Venice Biennale as a case study, this session explores how contemporary art responds to climate anxieties and disinformation, opening new spaces for dialogue and collective reflection. Participants will be invited to engage in experimental, creative activities that confront eco-anxiety, using AI-powered tools to spark conversation and insight. The proposal emphasizes the value of AI literacy as an essential skill for understanding and navigating the evolving relationships between technology, society, and the environment through the development of contemporary art practices through Media Artivism.

# About the speaker

Carolina Fernández-Castrillo is Associate Professor of AI and Digital Innovation at Carlos III University of Madrid and media artivist curator. Currently, she is the coordinator of Digital Communication and Culture at the Spanish Association of Media Research (AE-IC ), she leads the ecomedia literacy area at the Climate Strategic Initiative (UC3M), and the research on climate disinformation at Iberifier (Iberian Digital Media Observatory).
