# Abstract

The workshop offers an introduction to the use of Omeka Classic as an environment for designing small digital collections and digital exhibitions in the field of Digital and Public History. Participants will work with a set of heterogeneous digital objects (images, texts, documents, audiovisual materials) to reflect on how historical sources can be transformed into cultural resources accessible to a broad public. Through guided activities, the workshop will address item creation and basic metadata description (Dublin Core), curatorial organization through thematic collections, and the construction of short narrative paths that connect data, historical interpretation, and audiences. The aim is to show how Omeka Classic can be used as a prototyping tool for Digital and Public History projects. The workshop concludes with the creation of a small digital exhibition, reusable in teaching, research, or cultural heritage dissemination contexts.

# About the speaker

Emmanuela Carbé is Associate Professor of Contemporary Italian Literature at Ca’ Foscari University of Venice and a member of the Venice Centre for Digital and Public Humanities. Her research combines authorial philology and digital humanities. For nine years, she taught Digital Libraries and has led several workshops on Omeka. She coordinated the BiGraFo project, dedicated to Franco Fortini’s bibliography and based, for its user interface, on Omeka S. On born-digital archives, she published "Digitale d’autore. Macchine, archivi, letterature" (FUP-USiena Press, 2023) and coordinates ALDiNa – Archivi Letterari Digitali Nativi.

Mariangela Giglio is a PhD candidate in Cultural Heritage in the Digital Ecosystem at the University of Bologna. Her research project, devoted to the application of forensic methodologies to hybrid literary archives, takes Franco Fortini’s archive as its case study. She was also part of the BiGraFo project and is a member of the ALDiNa research group.
